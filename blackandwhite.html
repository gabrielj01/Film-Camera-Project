B&W

Introduction

Black and white photography, often abbreviated as B&W, is a form of photography that captures images without recording colors.
Instead, it relies solely on shades of gray, ranging from pure black to pure white.
Black and white photography has a timeless and classic quality, and it can be used to emphasize composition, form, and contrast.

Film Selection

Film Type: Black and white film comes in various types and sensitivities (ISO/ASA). Different films offer different grain structures, contrast levels, and tonal qualities.
Loading Film: In analog (film) photography, the photographer loads a roll of black and white film into the camera before taking pictures.

Short Brief

Black and white photography allows for a focus on the interplay of light and shadow, shapes, and textures without the distraction of color.
It has a rich history and is still appreciated for its ability to convey mood, emotion, and a sense of timelessness.
Many photographers continue to use black and white film or simulate the look digitally to create striking and evocative images.
