What is ISO ?

ISO, which stands for International Organization for Standardization, is a measure of your camera's sensitivity to light.
In the context of film cameras, it represents the sensitivity of the film to light.
In digital cameras, it refers to the sensitivity of the image sensor.

ISO Ratings

Low ISO (e.g., ISO 100 or 200):

Best suited for well-lit conditions. Produces images with fine detail and minimal grain.
Ideal for bright outdoor scenes with lots of natural light.

Medium ISO (e.g., ISO 400 or 800):

Offers a balanced sensitivity for varying lighting conditions.
Good for overcast days or indoor settings with moderate available light. Strikes a balance between detail and sensitivity.

High ISO (e.g., ISO 1600 and above):

Reserved for low-light situations. Increases sensitivity to capture more light in dim environments.
However, higher ISO settings may introduce more noticeable grain or noise, affecting image quality.

Adjusting ISO

1)When to Use Low ISO:

In well-lit scenes to maintain optimal image quality. During daylight hours or outdoor shoots with abundant natural light.

2)When to Use Medium ISO:

In situations with moderate lighting, like overcast days or indoor settings.
Balances sensitivity and detail for versatile shooting conditions.

3)When to Use High ISO:

In low-light conditions such as evening events or indoor scenes with limited light.
Sacrifices some image quality for the sake of capturing a properly exposed image in challenging lighting.

Auto ISO

Many modern cameras feature an "Auto ISO" setting. In this mode,
the camera adjusts ISO automatically based on the available light, providing convenience in dynamic lighting situations.

Experimenting with ISO

Understanding ISO sensitivity empowers you to control the exposure of your photos.
Experiment with different ISO settings in various lighting conditions to observe their impact on image quality
and choose the appropriate setting for your desired outcome.

Conclusion

By grasping the nuances of ISO sensitivity,
photographers can optimize their camera settings to capture the best possible images in diverse shooting environments.
